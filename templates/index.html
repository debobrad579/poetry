Dissociation

By: Ricki Cummings

I sit with another version
of myself eating this apple
and this apple. I see
myself bite, chew,
and swallow as I bite,
chew, and swallow. I cannot
read me, my other face
consumed in eating this apple.
In my sinus there is a buzzing
like a dying fluorescent light
that drowns this apple I’m eating.
I cannot tell if this other me
hears it, if I see me
seeing me chewing and lost.
I would be fine if I stayed confined
to this, to sitting and eating.
I grow concerned when I see me
on the train and getting
off at the wrong stop, leaving me
bewildered in the tunnel. Worried
when I see myself driving my car
and veering it into traffic. Alarmed
at the beach when I watch me
walk into the water calm with
stones in my pockets when there
are no stones in my pockets.
Curious when I sit and write
when I’ve had no pen in hand in weeks.
And every time I try to ask me
I’m gone before I get there.

He was born in Grand Forks, North Dakota in 1981. He graduated from Indiana-Purdue University in Fort Wayne in with a Creative Writing minor and an Interior Design associates’. The Product

By: Brady DeBoer

Above is a pixel art picture I made on a 128px by 128px digital canvas using the software: Aseprite. I used Python with Flask for the back end, HTML and CSS for the front end, and deployed everything to Heroku. I chose to create a pixel art image because that is an art form that I already new; I chose to display this all on a website because I new it wouldn't take long since I had already made 8 this past year, and it's more interesting than displaying everything on a google document. Now to my interpretation of the meaning: the speaker is sitting, and he is physically there, but he's not mentally there. The speaker is dissociating from his current activity, hence the name. When he tries to do things: to write, he is there, but his mind isn't; he's imagining another version of himself: another thing that could have happened or could happen. I related this to my own feelings of dissociation; I imagine myself closer to where I feel at home: to where I don't feel anxious. I imagine myself feeling happiness: looking towards the light; but in reality, I'm looking away: I'm not happy.
